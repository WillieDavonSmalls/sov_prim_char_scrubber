2 Permanent Dwelling (multi-family housing) ATC 2 is intended to model the entire building including the exterior shell (e.g., roof cover and cladding) and the interior structure (e.g, damage to drywall, flooring, cabinets, etc.). This occupancy type is applicable for modeling the structural damage to an apartment building that is owned by a landlord who rents out individual units to tenants. It is also applicable to modeling a content-only policy held by a tenant in such an apartment building.

40 Mortgage-backed Dwelling (Puerto Rico only) Caribbean Earthquake and Hurricane Models—ATC 40 represents residential structures covered by a Puerto Rico-specific policy related to the mortgage loan process where the limit is the mortgage amount, which decreases each year as the loan is paid off. Specifying ATC 40 in Puerto Rico will generate significantly lower loss estimates than specifying single-family dwelling (ATC 1 or ATC 41). Only structure is insured, not contents and appurtenant structures. The vulnerability curves reflect details specific to this policy, including average insurance-to-value conditions of the mortgage loan process. This should not be used for typical Homeowner's policies (that is, Dwelling-Fire). See FAQ: What Does The Occupancy Code ATC 40 Represent in RMS Models? for information about how ATC 40 is treated in Caribbean territories other than Puerto Rico.
40 General Residential Other Earthquake and Wind Models—In Mexico and Central America Hurricane Models, ATC 40 produces loss estimates that are identical to those obtained by specifying multi-family dwelling (ATC 2). In Mexico and Central and South America Earthquake Models other than Chile, ATC 40 is used to represent a general residential occupancy when the user does not know if it is single-family or multi-family dwelling. See FAQ: What Does The Occupancy Code ATC 40 Represent in RMS Models? for information about how ATC 40 is treated in other peril model regions, including Chile.
41 Homeowner Typical residential structure covered by policy that covers structure, contents, and appurtenant structures. Residential policies that are not tied to the mortgage process should use this occupancy. (Caribbean Hurricane model only)
42 Multi-Family Dwelling–Homeowners Association ATC 42 is intended to model only damage to the exterior shell (e.g., roof cover and cladding), as is the case for Homeowner Association policies. It can also be used to model losses to contents owned by the Homeowner Association (e.g., furniture in common areas, pool equipment, etc.).
43 Multi-Family Dwelling–Condominium Unit Owner ATC 43 is intended to model only damage to the interior structure (e.g., drywall, flooring, cabinets, etc.), as is the case for Condo Unit Owner (HO6) policies. It can also be used to model losses to contents owned by the unit owner.
44 Gasoline Service Station A gas station, which should include a gas station canopy as well as any small buildings on the premises.
47 Restaurants Restaurants tend to have a unique contents damageability once the envelop of the structure is breached in a wind event. Restaurants tend to have a higher susceptibility to damage than other entertainment classes of risk.
48 Casinos RMS received several claims datasets for losses to casinos along the Mississippi coast. In particular, casinos have unique business interruption dependencies that require a special occupancy code.
49 Acute Care Services Hospitals Hospital buildings that are used to provide acute care services. For other medical services facilities use ATC9.
50 OSHPD Acute Care Services Hospitals (California ONLY) Hospital buildings in California that are used to provide acute care services and are built or evaluated according to the Office of Statewide Health Planning and Development guidelines (OSHPD) guidelines.
51 Hotels - Large ATC 51 is intended to model time element losses of large and high-end hotels and luxury resorts. Structure and contents vulnerability curves for this occupancy are the same as the ones in ATC 3.
52 Hotels - Small and Medium ATC 52 is intended to model time element losses of small and medium size hotels and motels. Structure and contents vulnerability curves for this occupancy are the same as the ones in ATC 3.
53 Rental - General Commercial ATC 53 is intended to model loss of rental income of general commercial properties. Structure and contents vulnerability curves for this occupancy are the same as the ones in ATC 37.
54 Universities and Colleges Higher education structures do not tend to reflect the same type of damageability as elementary, intermediate, and secondary schools. Their structure is similar to other commercial occupancies. Since higher education structures are similar to other commercial occupancies, RMS recommends using ATC 54 for these structures rather than ATC 25, which incorporates higher vulnerability functions for education occupancy.
